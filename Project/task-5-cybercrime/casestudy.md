###### **Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**



**UPI fraud has emerged as a major cyber threat in India with the increasing use of digital payment platforms like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm. Unlike traditional cyberattacks, these scams do not require technical hacking skills; instead, they rely on deceiving individuals into approving transactions themselves. As per data shared in Parliament, more than 13 lakh cases of UPI fraud involving over ₹1,000 crore were reported during 2023–24, showing how widespread the problem has become.**



**These frauds usually follow a step-by-step approach. Initially, the victim is contacted through a phone call, text message, or WhatsApp by someone posing as a bank representative, delivery partner, or service agent. The fraudster creates pressure by mentioning urgent issues such as incomplete KYC verification or pending payments. After gaining the victim’s trust, they send a payment request or a fraudulent link and ask the user to enter their UPI PIN to “accept” money. However, entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment. In some situations, victims are further manipulated into revealing OTPs or installing remote access applications, allowing scammers to control their devices. For example, in Karnataka, a civil engineer reportedly lost more than ₹6 lakh through such deceptive transactions.**



**Young students, senior citizens, and individuals who are not very familiar with digital payment systems are often targeted.**

**The impact of such frauds is significant. Victims can lose large sums instantly, and recovering the money is often challenging. It also leads to anxiety and reduced confidence in online financial systems.**

**This case underlines the need for caution—users should never disclose their UPI PIN or OTP and must carefully verify every transaction request before proceeding.**
