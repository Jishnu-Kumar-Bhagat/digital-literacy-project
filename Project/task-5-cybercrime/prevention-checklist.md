###### **Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist**



1. **Verify the Source: Always check the sender’s full email address or profile. Scammers often use names that look official but come from fake IDs.**

2. **Think Before You Click: Do not click on suspicious links or attachments in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages from unknown sources.**

3. **Beware of Urgency: Messages that create panic (e.g., “Your account will be blocked”) are often scams designed to rush your decision.**

4. **Never Share Confidential Details: Do not share OTPs, passwords, or personal information with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank or company.**

5. **Never Pay for Jobs or Internships: Genuine companies do not ask for registration fees, training fees, or deposits to offer opportunities.**

6. **UPI Safety Rule: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money—PIN is only required when sending money.**

7. **Check Payment Requests Carefully: Always verify collect requests, QR codes, and payment links before approving any transaction.**

8. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Transactions: Do not access banking or payment apps on unsecured public networks.**

9. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security to your email, banking, and social media accounts.**

10. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Create complex passwords and avoid reusing them across multiple platforms.**

11. **Report Suspicious Activity: If you suspect fraud, immediately report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930.**
